The Essence

Defining the science of heat and transformation.

Thermochemistry is the study of the heat energy associated with chemical reactions and physical transformations. Whether a reaction releases energy (exothermic) or absorbs it (endothermic), thermochemistry quantifies these invisible forces, bridging the gap between molecular interactions and observable physics.

Systems & Surroundings

How we define boundaries in the thermal universe.

Open System

An unrestricted boundary allowing the free exchange of both matter and energy with the surroundings.

Closed System

A selective boundary that permits the flow of energy (heat/work) but keeps matter contained.

Isolated System

A theoretical perfect barrier where neither matter nor energy can escape or enter the system.

Enthalpy Universe

Mapping the various states of thermal content.

ΔHf

Standard Enthalpy of Formation

Heat change when one mole of a substance forms from elements in their standard states.

ΔHc

Standard Enthalpy of Combustion

Energy released when one mole of a substance undergoes complete combustion.

ΔHn

Enthalpy of Neutralization

Heat evolved during the reaction of an acid and a base to form water.
